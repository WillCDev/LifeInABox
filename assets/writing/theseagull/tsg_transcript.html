I see a seagull on the horizon,
Its wings flap in the breeze
As it circles the air above the ocean.

I see it as it dives like a dart into the waves.
Seconds later it comes up to the surface,
Some poor morsel of fish caught in its hungry beak.

I see him as he scurries and flurries in the air
With his mates as they haggle for food.
I see him fly off into the distance,
Probably to pastures anew 
On far off shores.
